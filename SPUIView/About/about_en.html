Mobile SpeedPro evaluates video quality using Huawei U-vMOS criteria.

Based on the long-term user behavior analysis in Huawei iLAB, it is found that unified criteria should be established to evaluate video experience on various screens in diverse network scenarios.

Combining ergonomics experimentation, sample surveys, and technology research, Huawei iLAB comes up with the top 3 factors in all-scenario video experience and released the video experience evaluation criteria User-experience-centric Video Mean Opinion Score (U-vMOS). The top 3 factors are video quality (sQuality), interactive experiences (sInteraction), and viewing experiences (sView).

During video playback, the network bandwidth and E2E delay affect the loading speed and fluency. Playing high-definition videos requires higher network performance. SpeedPro can get a scaled U-vMOS score of 1 to 5 to evaluate network readiness for video services, where 5 is Excellent, 4 Good, 3 Fair, 2 Poor, and 1 Bad.

SpeedPro tests 1080p videos by default. If a terminal or the live network does not support 1080P video testing, using 720P or 480P videos instead is recommended to get a more accurate test result.

Given the same initial buffering time and video freeze rate, the playback duration determines a U-vMOS score. By default, a video is played for one minute, which can be adjusted in line with actual requirements.

A video starts to play only after a specified amount of video data has been downloaded. Therefore, the amount of video data to be buffered determines the initial buffering time on a video client under the same network condition. Currently, this setting varies with OTT video vendors. SpeedPro uses the buffered data volume of about 2 seconds, which is the typical value for mainstream video clients.

Key indicators of a video test:

Indicator Description
U-vMOS Score A U-vMOS score is calculated by subtracting the damaged values of sInteraction and sView from the sQuality value. The obtained U-vMOS score may be lower than either sInteraction or sView value. The calculation formula is as follows: U-vMOS = (sQuality - 1)·(1 – α · (5 - sInteraction) – β (5 - sView) ) + 1 α and β indicate the sInteraction and sView damage coefficients, respectively, which are embedded into the U-vMOS algorithm library. Videos with different resolutions have different maximum U-vMOS scores. The following table lists the maximum U-vMOS scores at different resolutions on a 42-inch screen:

sView Score The sView score is determined by the video freeze rate. A higher video rate indicates a lower sView score if the sInteraction scores are the same. The calculation formula is as follows: Video freeze rate = Video freeze duration/(Playback time + Video freeze duration + Initial loading time)

sQuality Score The sQuality score is determined by the video resolution, bit rate, frame rate, and screen size. A larger bit rate indicates a higher sQuality score, and the maximum bit rate is determined by the video resolution and frame rate. A video resolution increases with the maximum bit rate. If a video resolution is not changed and the frame rate improves by two folds, the bit rate can increase by 1.6 folds. A larger screen size requires a higher resolution. If a low-resolution video is played on a large screen, a low sQuality score will be obtained.
sInteraction Score The sInteraction score is determined by the initial buffer time. A longer buffer time leads to a lower sInteraction score.

Initial Buffer Time Indicates from when a terminal sends an OTT program request to when the OTT cloud platform returns enough data for the terminal to display the first video image.
Stalling Time Indicates the total video stall durations during video viewing.
Stalling Times Indicates the number of video stalls during video viewing.
Max Download Speed Indicates the peak video download rate per second.
Bit Rate Indicates the number of bits sent within the specified period during video transmission.
Frame Rate Indicates the number of images displayed each second. A frame is a static image, and continuous frames constitute an animation.
Resolution Indicates the number of pixels in an image.
Screen Size The default value is 42 inches, the minimum screen size for 4K video playback, which best simulates actual video playback for 4K video U-vMOS calculation. You can choose Setting > Advanced Setting to modify the default value.
Video Test Duration The default value is 1 minute, which can be modified by choosing Setting > Advanced Setting.
